About the Lab

Developing computational methods for causal inference and interpretable AI with mathematical rigor.

Vision

The Computational Mathematics Lab develops rigorous computational methods at the intersection of mathematics, statistics, and artificial intelligence. Our goal is to create interpretable, theoretically sound frameworks for understanding causal relationships in complex systems.

Mission

We aim to bridge the gap between theoretical mathematical foundations and practical AI applications. Our research focuses on three core areas:

Causal Inference

Developing computational frameworks for causal discovery and inference in observational data

Structural Equation Modelling

Reducing bias in statistical methods for causal analysis in psychology and healthcare

Neural-Symbolic AI

Integrating symbolic reasoning with neural networks for interpretable AI systems

Research Philosophy

Mathematical Rigor

All our methods are grounded in sound mathematical theory with proofs and guarantees

Computational Efficiency

We develop scalable algorithms that work on real-world, high-dimensional data

Practical Impact

Our research addresses real problems in healthcare, finance, and social sciences

Open Science

All code, datasets, and papers are freely available for reproducibility
